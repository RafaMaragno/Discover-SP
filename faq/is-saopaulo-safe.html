Is São Paulo safe for tourists?

Short answer: Yes — São Paulo can be safe for tourists if you follow common-sense precautions: stay in well-reviewed areas, avoid isolated places after dark, keep valuables out of sight, and choose licensed transport.

Safe neighborhoods (overview)

  • Paulista / Bela Vista: Museums, shops, easy metro access.
  • Jardins: Upscale, restaurants and boutiques.
  • Vila Madalena: Nightlife and street art.
  • Itaim / Vila Olímpia: Business districts, safe and lively.

Transport tips

  • Use official taxi stands or ride-hailing apps (Uber, 99).
  • For groups or late arrivals, consider a private transfer.
  • Metro is safe during the day; avoid empty cars at night.

Money & valuables

  • Carry only needed cash; use cards where possible.
  • Keep phone and wallet in front pockets or zipped bags.
  • Use hotel safes for passports and valuables.

Quick FAQs

Is it safe to walk at night? Walking at night is fine in well-lit central areas; avoid deserted streets.

Can I drink tap water? Tap water is treated, but bottled water is often preferred.

Emergency numbers: Police/Fire/Ambulance: 190 / 192.
