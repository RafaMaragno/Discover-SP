Travel Tips for São Paulo

Planning a trip to São Paulo?
Here are the most common questions international visitors have, along with practical advice and trusted recommendations.

Is São Paulo safe for tourists?

São Paulo is a massive city (over 12 million people) with safe districts and areas to avoid at night. Stay in well-reviewed neighborhoods such as Paulista, Jardins, or Itaim Bibi, avoid empty streets after dark, keep valuables secure, carry minimal cash (cards widely accepted), and always use licensed or app-based transport.

How do I get from GRU Airport to downtown São Paulo?

GRU (Guarulhos) is 25 km from downtown. Main options include:

  • Private Transfer: Reliable and safe, ideal for late-night arrivals or with luggage.
  • Taxi / Ride Apps: Official airport taxis or ride-hailing apps like Uber and 99.
  • Airport Bus: Cheaper, connects to Paulista and Barra Funda.
  • Train + Metro: Very cheap, not ideal with bags.

How can I buy a SIM card in São Paulo?

Prepaid SIMs are sold by Vivo, TIM, and Claro. Bring your passport for registration. For convenience, activate an eSIM via services like Airalo or Yesim before arrival.

Quick Tips

Is it safe to walk at night?

Walking is fine in busy areas such as Paulista, Jardins, and Vila Madalena, but avoid dark side streets. When unsure, call a ride app.

Can I drink tap water?

Water is treated, but pipes and tanks may be poorly maintained. Safer to drink filtered or bottled water.

What number to call in an emergency?

Police: 190 / Ambulance: 192 / Fire: 193.
Keep your address written in Portuguese for emergencies.
